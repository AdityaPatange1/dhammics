A common misunderstanding: that the Eightfold Path is a ladder — rung one, then rung two, and
so on, until liberation. In practice it works more like a mandala. Each factor supports the
others; weakness in one appears as distortion in the rest.

## Three trainings, eight factors

The path is traditionally grouped into three trainings:

- **Wisdom (paññā)** — right view and right intention
- **Ethics (sīla)** — right speech, right action, right livelihood
- **Stillness (samādhi)** — right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration

Ethics steadies the life. Stillness steadies the mind. Wisdom sees, and the seeing loosens
the grip of craving that caused the unsteadiness in the first place.

## Right view

Right view is not a belief about cosmology. It is the willingness to see what is actually
happening: that things are changing, that clinging hurts, that there is a way the mind can
relate to experience that does not cause harm. Without this view we practise in circles.

## Right intention

Out of right view comes right intention — the inward slope of the heart toward renunciation,
goodwill, and non-harming. Intentions are not the final outcome; they are the compass.

## Right speech, action, livelihood

The ethical factors are not a list of rules imposed from outside. They are the recognition
that how we speak, how we act, and how we make our living shape the mind we bring to the
cushion. A life of dishonesty cannot settle into stillness; the mind remembers.

## Right effort, mindfulness, concentration

Here the path turns inward. Right effort is the gentle tending — neither slack nor forced —
of what is skilful. Right mindfulness is the clear knowing of what is present. Right
concentration is the gathered steadiness that lets wisdom finally cut.

> "Just as the ocean has one taste, the taste of salt, this Dhamma has one taste: the taste
> of freedom." — *Udana 5.5*

## An everyday experiment

For a single day, lay the eight factors beside your life:

1. Is my **view** being coloured by a craving I haven't noticed?
2. What **intention** is moving in the background as I act?
3. Is my **speech** true, kind, timely, useful?
4. What am I doing that I wouldn't want seen?
5. Does my **livelihood** quietly contradict what I value?
6. Where could a small adjustment of **effort** unhook me?
7. Am I actually **present**, or am I rehearsing a past/future?
8. Where does the mind feel **collected** today?

You will not solve all eight. You are not supposed to. Simply turning the light on each in
turn is already the path walking itself.

*Paticca-samuppada* — literally "arising in dependence" — is the most structural of the
Buddha's teachings and, at first glance, the most intimidating. Twelve links, spinning
three lifetimes, lighting up diagrams in a hundred commentaries. It is also the teaching he
considered the deepest.

The good news is that we do not have to understand every link on first encounter. We only
have to understand the *shape* of the insight.

## The shape

> "When this is, that is. From the arising of this, that arises. When this is not, that is
> not. From the ceasing of this, that ceases." — *Samyutta Nikaya 12.21*

That is the whole thing, compressed. Nothing that arises arises without causes. Nothing that
seems solid stands on its own. *And* — this is the hinge — because all suffering is
conditioned, it can be conditioned otherwise.

## The twelve links, quickly

The classical formulation runs:

1. **Ignorance** (avijjā)
2. conditions **formations** (saṅkhārā)
3. conditioning **consciousness** (viññāṇa)
4. conditioning **name-and-form** (nāma-rūpa)
5. conditioning the **six sense bases** (saḷāyatana)
6. conditioning **contact** (phassa)
7. conditioning **feeling** (vedanā)
8. conditioning **craving** (taṇhā)
9. conditioning **clinging** (upādāna)
10. conditioning **becoming** (bhava)
11. conditioning **birth** (jāti)
12. conditioning **aging-and-death** (jarāmaraṇa), with all that follows.

Reverse the sequence and you have a path: undo ignorance, and the whole chain unspools.

## Where to actually work

The commentaries rightly note that the link between **feeling** and **craving** is where
practice lives. A pleasant feeling arises; craving leans toward it. An unpleasant feeling
arises; craving leans away. This leaning is the moment where the chain tightens — and where,
with mindfulness, it can loosen.

We do not have to meditate our way through all twelve links to taste cessation. We have to
learn to notice *one* feeling, *one* leaning, *one* time, and let it not become a clinging.

## A contemplation

Sit for ten minutes. Watch any one unpleasant sensation — an ache in the knee, a cool draft.

- Notice the **feeling** (vedanā).
- Notice the **craving** that immediately arises — usually, the wish for it to stop.
- Notice any **clinging** that follows — stories about why this sensation is intolerable.
- Then simply watch the sensation change. (It will.)
- Watch the craving change. (It will.)
- Watch the stories change. (They will.)

The arithmetic of suffering, done once, in slow motion. That is enough for today.

## Why it matters

Dependent origination is sometimes called the Buddha's most technical teaching. It is also
his kindest. It says: *nothing about you is fixed*. The tight knot you call a self is being
re-tied, moment by moment, by causes you are learning to see. Each moment of clear seeing is
a knot that does not get re-tied.

That is the entire path, restated one more time.
